Shopify Product Recommendation Quizzes

Designed and developed interactive product recommendation quizzes for multiple Shopify e-commerce brands, helping customers discover products tailored to their preferences while improving engagement and conversion rates. I was responsible for the end-to-end experience, from UX planning and visual design to implementation, testing, and developer collaboration.

These quizzes served as personalized shopping assistants, guiding users toward relevant products through engaging question flows across industries such as Food & Beverage, Furniture & Home Furnishings, Beauty & Personal Care, Fashion & Apparel, Home & Interior Design, and Consumer Retail.

The Challenge

Each brand had unique business goals, customer behaviors, and product catalogs, requiring tailored recommendation experiences.

Some clients already had quizzes in place, but they suffered from low completion rates, unclear user journeys, poor visual design, and weak conversion performance. Others needed entirely new quiz experiences designed from the ground up.

The challenge was to quickly understand different industries, identify opportunities for improvement, and create engaging experiences that encouraged users to complete the quiz and confidently purchase recommended products.

My Role & Responsibility

I led the design and delivery of quiz experiences across multiple Shopify brands:

Led the end-to-end design and delivery of product recommendation quizzes

Designed responsive quiz experiences for desktop, tablet, and mobile devices

Revamped existing low-performing quizzes to improve usability and conversions

Created new quiz experiences from scratch based on business goals and customer needs

Built quizzes using AskWhai (Visual Quiz Builder)

Collaborated directly with clients, developers, and cross-functional teams throughout the project lifecycle

Supported implementation through HTML, CSS, and SCSS enhancements

Conducted testing and quality assurance to ensure a seamless user experience

Learnings

  • Quiz UX is deceptively nuanced - question sequencing, progress indication, and result logic all directly affect completion rates and perceived value

  • Revamping underperforming quizzes taught me to lead with data: identifying drop-off points before redesigning ensured changes were targeted, not cosmetic

  • Working across six brands in parallel sharpened my ability to context-switch rapidly while keeping each brand's tone and visual identity distinct

  • Direct client collaboration across time zones improved my written communication and async feedback skills - clarity in documentation became as important as clarity in design

  • Hands-on HTML/CSS/SCSS work during implementation gave me a much deeper appreciation for the gap between design intent and browser reality, and how to close it

  • Building quizzes inside a no-code tool (AskWhai) taught me to design within platform constraints creatively, finding workarounds that preserved UX quality

  • Testing across devices and browsers reinforced that responsive design is not a final step but an ongoing consideration woven throughout the design process
